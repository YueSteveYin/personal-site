Origin Story

My journey into tech didn’t start with textbooks—it started with chaos and curiosity. Back in elementary school, while other kids were memorizing multiplication tables, I was deep into Redstone circuits and Scratch games. I built mini-games, designed maps, and wrote basic logic and functions. It blew my mind that I could actually build my own world—code could do things that even adults couldn’t figure out. That was the hook.

The catch? I never finished a single map. Not once. Every time I got halfway through, my mom would find out, delete everything, and deliver the classic combo: scolding plus a bit of “parental feedback.” But instead of quitting, that just fueled my obsession. Apparently, nothing sparks an interest in computer engineering quite like losing your work and your Wi-Fi privileges in the same afternoon.

I didn’t choose software engineering because I thought I was some genius—I chose it because I’m genuinely in love with it. I can spend nights (and entire weekends) debugging one stubborn function, and somehow, it still feels worth it. That tiny thrill when something finally works? Better than coffee. Almost better than sleep. Almost.

I believe in building with purpose, learning with focus, and growing with consistency. Right now, I’m seeking opportunities in software development—looking to contribute what I’ve got, keep leveling up, and turn inspiration into systems that matter.
